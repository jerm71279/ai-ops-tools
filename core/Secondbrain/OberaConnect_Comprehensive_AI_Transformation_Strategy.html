The Director Mindset Applied to MSP Tasks

The director mindset transforms how you approach every task. Instead of doing the work yourself, you design the outcome you want and direct AI to execute. Your deep network knowledge becomes the strategic input; AI handles the tactical execution.

Task Director Approach
New Site Deployment "Generate a complete Ubiquiti configuration for a 50-user office with 3 VLANs (corporate, guest, IoT), guest WiFi with captive portal, SonicWall integration for firewall rules, using our standard naming conventions. Include switch port assignments and wireless coverage recommendations." → Review → Validate → Deploy
Troubleshooting "Customer reports intermittent WiFi drops on the 5GHz band during business hours. Here's the UniFi controller export and recent event logs. Analyze the patterns, correlate with device density and channel utilization, and provide top 3 most likely causes with specific diagnostic steps for each."
Azure Migration "Create a comprehensive migration plan for moving this customer's on-premises file server (500GB, 25 users) to Azure Files. Include timeline with milestones, all prerequisites, Azure VPN configuration for hybrid access, permission mapping strategy, rollback procedures, and user communication templates for each phase."
Security Review "Analyze this SonicWall configuration against NIST Cybersecurity Framework. Identify gaps in each function (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover), prioritize by risk severity, and generate specific remediation steps including the exact rule changes needed."
Documentation "Generate complete network documentation from this discovery scan. Include network topology description, IP addressing schema with VLAN assignments, device inventory with firmware versions, security group assignments, and maintenance recommendations."
Customer Proposal "Draft a proposal for upgrading [Customer]'s network from their current MikroTik setup to Ubiquiti. Include executive summary, current state assessment based on our last site visit notes, proposed architecture, implementation timeline, cost breakdown, and ROI projection based on typical support reduction."

Building Director Playbooks

Create standardized "director playbooks" that document how to direct AI for common tasks. These playbooks become organizational assets that any team member can use to achieve consistent, high-quality results.

Playbook Structure

  1. Task Definition: What outcome are we trying to achieve?
  2. Required Inputs: What information does AI need to do this well?
  3. Prompt Template: The specific direction to give AI, with placeholders for variables
  4. Quality Checklist: What to verify before accepting the output
  5. Common Refinements: Typical follow-up prompts needed to perfect the output

Key Insight: The skill shifts from knowing how to configure to knowing what outcome you need. Your years of network experience don't become obsolete—they become more valuable because you can direct AI with precision that less experienced practitioners cannot match.

The Three-Step Data Moat Strategy

Step 1: Clean Your Data

Before AI can extract value, data must be consistent and complete:

  1. Audit NinjaOne for duplicate customer records, inconsistent naming, and incomplete fields
  2. Standardize device naming conventions across all UniFi controllers
  3. Ensure ticket categorization is consistent—create and enforce category taxonomy
  4. Consolidate configuration templates and document the "why" behind design decisions
  5. Systematically capture customer preferences and business context in CRM notes

Step 2: AI Analyzes and Correlates

Once data is clean, AI can surface insights humans would miss:

  1. "Which Ubiquiti models have the highest failure rates in our customer base?"
  2. "What configuration patterns correlate with fewer support tickets over 6 months?"
  3. "Which customers are likely to need capacity upgrades based on growth trajectory?"
  4. "What time-of-day and day-of-week patterns exist in our ticket volume?"
  5. "Which customer types generate the highest margins vs. most support burden?"

Step 3: AI Recommends Next Actions

The insights become proactive recommendations:

  1. Proactive customer recommendations before problems occur ("Based on patterns, this AP will need replacement in 60 days")
  2. Automated identification of upsell/cross-sell opportunities ("This customer's bandwidth usage suggests Azure migration")
  3. Predictive staffing based on ticket volume patterns ("Next Tuesday will be 40% heavier than average")
  4. Configuration improvements pushed to similar customer environments ("This fix reduced tickets by 60% at similar sites")

The Moat: Competitors can copy your service offerings tomorrow. They cannot copy years of operational data, learned customer patterns, and configuration wisdom fed into AI. Each day you operate, the moat deepens.

The 98/2 Principle Applied

The goal is not to automate everything—it's to automate the predictable so humans can focus on the exceptional. For each back-office function, identify what's routine vs. what requires judgment.

Function AI Handles (98%) Human Audits (2%)
Finance Invoice generation from time entries, expense categorization and coding, payment processing and reminders, bank reconciliation, standard financial reporting Unusual expense approvals, new vendor onboarding, non-standard pricing decisions, budget variance analysis, strategic financial planning
HR/Admin PTO balance tracking and approval routing, policy question responses, onboarding checklist management, training schedule coordination, standard approvals Performance issues requiring conversation, hiring decisions, policy changes, employee relations matters, conflict resolution
Procurement Standard equipment orders from approved vendors, license renewal processing, routine vendor communications, inventory level monitoring New vendor evaluation and selection, large purchase justification, contract negotiation, strategic sourcing decisions
Customer Ops Contract renewal reminders and processing, standard service request handling, usage report generation, routine customer communications, scheduling Escalated customer issues, custom pricing negotiations, at-risk account interventions, strategic relationship management
Support Triage Ticket categorization and priority assignment, initial response with KB suggestions, technician routing based on skills, SLA monitoring and alerts Complex technical issues requiring expertise, VIP customer escalations, novel problems not in knowledge base, customer complaints

Implementation: Codify Rules as Automation

Your Azure expansion provides the foundation for autonomous back-office operations through Power Automate and Logic Apps:

  1. Connect Data Sources: Integrate NinjaOne, accounting systems, and operational tools with AI for real-time monitoring and action
  2. Write Policies as Prompts: "When a P1 ticket arrives after hours, escalate immediately and notify on-call. When a standard renewal is due, generate invoice and initiate reminder sequence."
  3. Build Exception Triggers: AI processes routine cases automatically; flags exceptions with full context for human review
  4. Create Audit Dashboards: Leadership sees exception reports, not transaction details—focus on the 2% that matters
  5. Close the Loop: AI executes, documents, and reports. No bottlenecks waiting for human approval on routine matters
