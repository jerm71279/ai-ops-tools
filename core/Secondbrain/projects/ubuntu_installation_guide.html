Guide: Wiping a PC and Installing Ubuntu from a Bootable USB

Please follow these steps carefully. This process will erase everything on your mini PC's hard drive.

1. Boot from the USB Drive

  1. Plug the bootable USB drive into your mini PC.
  2. Power on the mini PC and immediately start pressing the key to enter the BIOS/UEFI settings. Common keys are F2, F10, F12, Del, or Esc. The correct key is often displayed on the screen for a brief moment when the computer starts.
  3. In the BIOS/UEFI settings, find the "Boot" or "Boot Order" menu.
  4. Change the boot order to make the USB drive the first boot device. It might be listed as "USB HDD," "Removable Device," or the name of the USB drive itself.
  5. Save your changes and exit the BIOS/UEFI settings. The computer will restart.

2. Start the Ubuntu Installation

  1. The mini PC should now boot from the USB drive and you will see the Ubuntu installer.
  2. On the welcome screen, select "Install Ubuntu".
  3. Follow the on-screen prompts to select your language and keyboard layout.

3. Wipe the Disk and Install Ubuntu

  1. You will reach a step called "Installation type". This is the most important step for wiping your old Red Hat installation.
  2. Choose the option "Erase disk and install Ubuntu".
    • WARNING: This will delete all programs, documents, photos, music, and any other files on your mini PC's internal hard drive.
  3. Click "Install Now". You will see a final confirmation window showing the changes that will be made to the disk. Click "Continue" to proceed.

4. Complete the Installation

  1. The installer will now copy the Ubuntu files to the hard drive.
  2. While it's installing, you will be asked to:
    • Choose your geographical location (timezone).
    • Create your user account by choosing a username and a strong password.
  3. When the installation is finished, you will see a message saying "Installation Complete". Click "Restart Now".

5. First Boot into Ubuntu

  1. You will be prompted to remove the installation medium (your USB drive) and press Enter.
  2. Your mini PC will restart and boot into your new Ubuntu installation.

You have now successfully replaced Red Hat with Ubuntu on your mini PC.
